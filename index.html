1. Introduction

Focus Cat ("we," "our," or "the extension") is a privacy-first productivity Chrome extension that uses local artificial intelligence to help users maintain focus during work or study sessions[cite: 6]. This Privacy Policy explains our data practices and your privacy rights.

2. Information We DO NOT Collect

Focus Cat is designed with privacy as the core principle[cite: 6]. We explicitly DO NOT collect, store, transmit, or process:

  • Webcam Recordings: Your camera feed is analyzed in real-time using local AI and immediately discarded[cite: 6]. No frames are saved, recorded, or transmitted[cite: 6].
  • Screenshots or Images: No visual data from your camera is ever saved to disk or memory beyond the milliseconds needed for AI analysis.
  • Browsing History: We do not track, monitor, or collect information about websites you visit[cite: 6].
  • Location Data: No GPS coordinates, IP addresses, or location information[cite: 6].
  • Device Information: No personal device identifiers, hardware specifications, or personal system information.
  • Third-Party Data Sharing: We do not sell or share personal data. Anonymous interaction metrics are processed purely to improve user experience[cite: 4].

3. Information We Store Locally

The ONLY data stored by Focus Cat is saved locally in your browser using Chrome's storage API and never leaves your device[cite: 4, 6]:

3.1 Productivity Statistics

  • Daily Time Counters: Three anonymous numbers representing seconds spent in each state (focused, distracted, away from desk)[cite: 4].

3.2 User Preferences

  • Detection Mode: Your selected monitoring mode preference (laptop/phone , future aspect for new features)[cite: 4].

3.3 Anonymous Identification

  • Client ID: A randomly generated unique string stored locally to differentiate separate user sessions across analytics events anonymously[cite: 4].

Important: All local data is stored in your local browser storage (chrome.storage.local)[cite: 4]. This local history data never leaves your computer, is not synchronized across devices, and is not accessible to us or any third party[cite: 6].

4. How the Extension Works

4.1 Local AI Processing

Focus Cat uses TensorFlow.js and Teachable Machine libraries that run entirely in your browser[cite: 1, 6]:

  • AI models are downloaded once during installation and stored locally[cite: 6]
  • Your webcam feed is processed frame-by-frame in your browser's memory[cite: 1, 6]
  • Each frame is analyzed and immediately discarded (within milliseconds)[cite: 1, 6]
  • Only the AI's classification result (focused/distracted/away) is used, not the image itself[cite: 1, 4]
  • No frames are sent to external servers or cloud services[cite: 6]

4.2 Webcam Permission

Focus Cat requests webcam permission solely for local AI focus detection[cite: 4, 6]:

  • The camera activates only when you click "Start Focus Session"[cite: 1]
  • The video feed is displayed to you as a preview for transparency[cite: 1, 6]
  • The feed is processed locally using on-device machine learning[cite: 6]
  • No recording, saving, or transmission of video data occurs[cite: 6]
  • You can revoke camera permission at any time in Chrome settings[cite: 4, 6]

6. Third-Party Services

  • No personal data, browser footprints, tracking cookies, or video frames are sent to Google Analytics[cite: 4, 6].
  • No advertisement networks or personal demographic trackers are integrated[cite: 6].

Telemetry Architecture: Focus Cat contains no backend databases or custom infrastructure. Outbound network communication is limited to secure analytics logging intended to discover bugs and evaluate usage trends[cite: 4, 6].
